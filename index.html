What Makes a Strong Password?

  • A password should be 16 characters or more; research has found that 45 percent of Americans use passwords of eight characters or less, which are not as secure as longer passwords.
  • Have at least 1 uppercase
  • Have at least 1 lowercase
  • Have at least 1 special character " ! @ # $ % ^ & * _ - + = ~ ` : ; " ' < > , . ? / \ | ( ) "
  • Have at least 1 uppercase
  • Passwords should not be used in multiple places
  • Passwords should not include personal information
  • Passwords should not contain any consecutive letters or numbers
  • Passwords should not be the word “password” or the same letter or number repeated
